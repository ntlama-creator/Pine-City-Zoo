GORILLA

Gorillas are the largest living primates and are native to the forests of central Africa. They are ground-dwelling and primarily herbivorous, feeding on leaves, stems, and fruit. Gorillas are highly social animals, living in groups led by a dominant male known as a silverback.

There are two species of gorillas: the eastern gorilla and the western gorilla. Both species are critically endangered due to habitat loss, poaching, and disease. Conservation efforts are underway to protect these magnificent animals and their habitats.

Gorillas are known for their intelligence and complex social structures. They communicate through a variety of vocalizations, gestures, and facial expressions. Despite their size and strength, gorillas are generally gentle and shy animals.
