GIRAFFE

Giraffes are the tallest land animals, known for their long necks and legs. They are native to Africa and inhabit savannahs and open woodlands. Giraffes are herbivores, primarily feeding on leaves, fruits, and flowers from trees.

Their long necks allow them to reach high branches, giving them access to food sources that other herbivores cannot reach. Giraffes have a unique coat pattern, with irregular brown patches on a lighter background, which helps them blend into their surroundings.

Giraffes are social animals and live in loose herds. They are known for their gentle demeanor and are often seen interacting with one another.
