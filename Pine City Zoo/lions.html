LION

Lions are large carnivorous mammals that belong to the family Felidae. They are known for their majestic appearance, with males sporting a distinctive mane. Lions are social animals, living in groups called prides, which typically consist of related females, their cubs, and a small number of adult males.

These powerful predators primarily hunt large ungulates, such as zebras and wildebeests, using teamwork and strategy to bring down their prey. Lions are also known for their impressive vocalizations, including roars that can be heard over long distances.

Lions are found in a variety of habitats, including savannahs, grasslands, and open woodlands. They are most commonly associated with the African continent, although a small population of Asiatic lions exists in the Gir Forest of India.
