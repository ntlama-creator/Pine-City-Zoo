GEMSBOKS

Gemsboks are large antelopes found in the arid regions of southern Africa. They are known for their striking appearance, with long, straight horns and a distinctive black and white facial pattern. Gemsboks are well adapted to their harsh environment, able to withstand high temperatures and go for long periods without water.

Gemsboks are herbivores, primarily grazing on grasses and browsing on shrubs. They are social animals, often forming herds of up to 20 individuals. During the hottest parts of the day, gemsboks seek shade and rest, becoming more active during the cooler morning and evening hours.

Gemsboks are herbivorous and can be found in different habitats including savannahs, grasslands, and arid regions. They prefer to stay near water sources.
