PANDA

Pandas are large bears native to China, known for their distinctive black and white coloring. They primarily inhabit bamboo forests, where they spend most of their time eating bamboo shoots and leaves. Pandas are solitary animals, and their diet consists almost entirely of bamboo, which makes up about 99% of their food intake.

Despite being classified as carnivores, pandas have adapted to a herbivorous diet. They have strong jaws and teeth that allow them to crush tough bamboo stalks. Pandas are also known for their playful behavior, often seen rolling around and climbing trees.

Pandas are considered a vulnerable species, with habitat loss and fragmentation posing significant threats to their survival. Conservation efforts are underway to protect their natural habitat and ensure their survival in the wild.
