TIGER

Tigers are the largest cat species in the world and are known for their striking orange coat with black stripes. They are native to Asia and can be found in a variety of habitats, including tropical forests, grasslands, and savannahs. Tigers are solitary hunters and are primarily active at night.

These magnificent animals are apex predators, preying on large ungulates such as deer and wild boar. Tigers are known for their powerful build and strong jaws, which allow them to take down prey much larger than themselves. They are also excellent swimmers and often hunt in water.

Tigers are classified as endangered, with habitat loss and poaching posing significant threats to their survival. Conservation efforts are underway to protect their natural habitat and ensure their survival in the wild.
