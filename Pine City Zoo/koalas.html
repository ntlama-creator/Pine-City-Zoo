KOALA

Koalas are marsupials native to Australia, known for their distinctive appearance and tree-dwelling habits. They have a stocky body, large head, and round, fluffy ears. Koalas are primarily herbivorous, feeding on eucalyptus leaves, which provide them with the necessary nutrients.

These animals are well-adapted to their arboreal lifestyle, with strong limbs and sharp claws for climbing. Koalas are mostly nocturnal, spending the majority of their time sleeping in trees to conserve energy.

Koalas are social animals and often live in groups called "clans." They communicate through a variety of vocalizations, including grunts and bellows.
